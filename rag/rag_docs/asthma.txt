Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition characterized by inflammation and narrowing of the airways.
Common symptoms include shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing, and chest tightness.
Triggers may include allergens, air pollution, respiratory infections, physical exertion, and stress.
Asthma severity varies from mild intermittent symptoms to severe persistent episodes.
Proper management includes avoiding triggers, using prescribed inhalers, and monitoring respiratory symptoms.
Early diagnosis and adherence to treatment plans can significantly improve quality of life.
